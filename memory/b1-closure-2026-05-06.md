- Вардек `lastSyncAt`: `2026-05-06T12:19:56Z` (507 min ago at watcher kill)
- Интерьер `lastSyncAt`: `2026-05-06T10:49:17Z` (597 min ago at watcher kill)
- System-wide sync coverage: `9 / 212` active accounts synced in last 20 min
- `cronHeartbeats.syncDispatch`: `отстаёт 13 мин`
- Both accounts: `status=active`, fresh tokens, `consecutiveSyncErrors=0`, `lastError=null`

Phase 6 conservative profile runs `syncDispatch` every 45 min processing a small subset of accounts per tick. With 212 active accounts and ~9 acc / 20-min, full coverage takes ~16h. Heavy accounts at the back of dispatcher queue can wait many hours for their turn.

This is **environment throughput throttling**, not a B1 correctness regression. Function-level verification proves the fix works end-to-end inside `internal.ruleEngine.getAccountAllAdIds`; the organic-tick path on the watched accounts is the next dependent step but cannot be observed on hour scale under Phase 6.

Direct-invocation E2E (calling `internal.ruleEngine.checkRulesForAccount`) was rejected because `checkRulesForAccount` has real `stopAd` / notify side effects and is gated behind business approval, not just operator approval. Per UZ-style policy: side-effecting automation only with explicit business go.

## Follow-up triggers (separate decisions, not auto-implied by B1)

### F1. Sync throughput bump

Phase 6 throughput is the bottleneck for B1 acceptance verification AND for user-visible rule freshness. With 212 active accounts and 16h full coverage, rules lag actual ad performance by hours.

Candidate parameters (require own design, acceptance, rollback):

- `SYNC_BATCH_SIZE_V2` 10 → 30 (3× per-tick coverage)
- `SYNC_WORKER_COUNT_V2` 1 → 2 (doubles throughput, doubles concurrent V8 slot usage)

Do NOT bundle with B1.
